Data Shows Widely Varying Levels of Mask Usage Throughout the USA

Patterns indicate masking-up is not a health choice but rather a political one

County by county data collected from the prelimary stages of the COVID-19 pandemic in the USA shows remarkable patterns in the willingness to wear a mask. The effects of the politicalization of this concept by the Trump administration is highly visible in the data collected thus far. Predominantly "Red" states and counties are less likely to always or frequently wear masks. Whereas predominantly "Blue" states and counties are more likely to always or frequently mask up. There are, of course, anamolies to this general trend.
